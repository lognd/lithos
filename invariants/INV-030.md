Checked mirror of INV-30 (Optimization reproducibility
and attribution) in `docs/spec/regolith/13-invariants.md`; that file
is the normative source.

Mechanism: `optimize_discrete` enumerates each `ChoicePoint`'s
candidates in declared order with a deterministic depth-first
backjumping walk; both discrete and continuous drivers derive their
entire search path from the caller's `seed` via one in-house,
platform-independent PRNG (`_splitmix64`). Every candidate is
evaluated only through the injected `Evaluator` (AD-22's private-
scoring-path ban).

Argument summary: determinism reduces to "the driver's call sequence
to `Evaluator` is a pure function of (domain, seed, budget)" composed
with "the evaluator's own output is a pure function of its argument"
(INV-10) composed with "the trace's serialization is a pure function
of the evaluation sequence" -- the composition of three pure functions
is pure.

Anchor (primary enforcing site): `python/regolith/orchestrator/optimize.py :: optimize_discrete`
