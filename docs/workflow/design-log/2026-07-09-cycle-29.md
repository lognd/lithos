## D156. Cost expiry clock: one `as_of` seam, deferrals never cached

No ambient build clock exists anywhere in the toolchain (the
realizer normalizes wall time out; adapter timeouts are the only
wall-clock use). Rather than invent one, the date enters costing at
exactly one seam (`load_cost_context`'s `as_of`, defaulting to
UTC-today there only); an expired pricing record yields a Deferral
naming the record -- never cached, never content-addressed, so
determinism and the evidence store stay clock-free. Recorded in
`orchestrator/costing.py`'s module docstring and WO-54's ledger.

## D157. Frame property resolution is orchestrator-side model input, never payload mutation

The frame payload is content-addressed at lowering (AD-18/AD-22) and
stays byte-stable; name-only `section`/`material` RecordRefs resolve
at the orchestrator boundary (`orchestrator/frame_resolve.py`,
std.civil-first, INV-22-style pinning) into model INPUTS for the
slice-C beam harness models -- the WO-54 estimator-inputs staging
shape, applied to frames. Finding recorded with it: no ratified
corpus design can reach a numeric utilization/deflection verdict via
record content alone, because every governing member declares
`section: free` -- a genuine L3 section-search variable (post-v1),
not missing stdlib content. The blanket `unsupported_op` deferrals
were replaced by specific reasons (`frame_section_free`,
`frame_member_not_found`, `no_frame_model`, `frame_load_untargeted`)
and the five deferral goldens pin them.

## D158. Solver interval corners are chosen explicitly and recorded (audit M3)

Feldspar's frame consumer solves load intervals at the conservative
`.hi` corner and records the corner choice in the result's
`assumptions`; lengths/geometry assert the producer's
degenerate-interval invariant (`lo == hi`) and are unit-normalized to
SI, honestly `OutOfDomain` otherwise. Silent lower-corner solves and
assumed-SI mixing are defects, not defaults, anywhere a safety
verdict consumes an interval.
