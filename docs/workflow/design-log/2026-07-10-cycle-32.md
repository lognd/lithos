## D186 (addendum, owner directive). Signal-integrity discipline: impedance, termination, stackup choice -- all calculated, all visible (charter 35)

Owner asks for on-board impedance matching, shunt/bypass ("high-pass
to ground") discipline on lines that matter, smart layer-count
choice, and every decision justified by VISIBLE numbers. Ruling
(charter `35-signal-integrity.md`; feldspar WO-25 models + lithos
WO-78 machinery):

- **Stackups are records** (`std.elec.stackups`): fab-published
  layer structures (count, dielectric heights, Dk, copper weights)
  under the AD-34 sourcing law -- fabs PUBLISH impedance stackups,
  so this family is citable at volume.
- **Impedance is a calculated claim**: `elec.impedance(<net>)
  within [lo, hi]` discharged by cited closed-form models
  (Hammerstad-Jensen microstrip, stripline, edge-coupled
  differential per IPC-2141-class formulas; feldspar-side, memo-
  calibrated against published fab calculators). Pre-layout the
  trace width/gap are ordinary `in [lo, hi]` slots THE OPTIMIZER
  SOLVES against the impedance claim (the boundary-finding form,
  D184); post-layout the extracted RealizedLayout geometry
  re-discharges the same claim (T2, the anti-staleness property).
- **Termination/bypass is a discipline, not folklore**: claim forms
  (`elec.termination(<net>, scheme=series|parallel|ac_shunt)`)
  sized by cited formulas (line Z, driver impedance, rise time ->
  R/C values with the arithmetic IN the evidence), plus AD-21
  `erc:` rules demanding bypass/termination presence on flagged
  net classes (every supply pin's shunt-C within a distance bound;
  every declared high-speed net carries its scheme) -- violations
  release-gated, numbers cited.
- **Layer count is a `by select`**: candidate stackup records
  declared, chosen by the discrete engine under a cost objective
  with feasibility = every impedance claim achievable on that
  stackup (+ rule-pack density screens); pinned with cause+trace.
  Full routability stays post-layout evidence, honestly.
- **Visibility**: evidence tables carry the calculation inputs/
  outputs; a per-board SI TABLE SHEET (DrawingModel `tables`, the
  schedule mechanism) renders net / target / stackup / width/gap /
  computed Zo / margin / cause -- browsable in graphite like every
  sheet; the parity report classes every sized value.

No new proof machinery anywhere: records + models + claim forms +
the existing engine + the existing table sheets. Mantra check:
Unambiguous (every number carries its formula citation + inputs),
Intent-based (declare the target impedance and the net class, not
the trace recipe), User-friendly (the SI sheet is the review
artifact).
