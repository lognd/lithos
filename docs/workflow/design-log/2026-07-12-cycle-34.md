## D212 (WO-101 execution). BOM v2 mass uses the pinned topology volume, not a ship-time STEP re-import

WO-101's brief (and charter 38 sec. 1.7) calls for "real mass =
record density x OCP volume over the pinned STEP bytes". Implementing
that literally -- re-importing the STEP bytes at ship time and running
`GProp_VolumeProperties` -- collides with a HARD guarantee: the
`RealizedGeometry` schema (and WO-22's acceptance) states plainly that
OCCT's STEP serialization is NOT byte-stable cross-platform, and names
`TopologySummary` the cross-platform determinism GOLDEN for exactly
that reason (INV-10/AD-6: no wall-clock, platform-stable hashed
artifacts). A second, ship-time GProp pass over the (platform-varying)
STEP bytes would produce a volume the byte-identical-golden rule
cannot depend on.

Resolution (in scope, recorded, not invented): the derived BOM uses
`TopologySummary.volume_mm3` -- which IS OCCT's own GProp volume of the
pinned STEP solid, computed once at realize time and pinned cross-
platform-stably in the IR -- and carries `step_content_hash` as the
row's geometry provenance pin. This satisfies "real mass = density x
OCCT volume, both pins carried" (density record pin + geometry pin)
WITHOUT re-deriving a non-deterministic number. If a future consumer
genuinely needs a fresh ship-time GProp (reopen criterion), it must
first establish a platform-stable STEP export, which is WO-22's own
deferred item -- not a BOM concern.

Residuals named (WO-101 close-out, all WO-shaped follow-ons, none
blocking the shipped core): (1) ship-time cost-estimate threading --
the derive/join logic + cost columns are complete and tested, but the
CLI ship wiring currently constructs the BOM backend with an empty
estimate map (honest empty cost cells with reasons) rather than
resolving `report.cost_estimates` digests from the payload store; (2)
the CAM plan summary schedule (`cost_schedule.py` ships the cost
summary + calcite member schedule; the CAM plan has no backend-facing
payload surface yet); (3) full three-corpus ship goldens
(cnc_router_r1 / timber_pavilion / espresso end-to-end) -- the
derivation, mass, cost-join, unsourced, determinism, and sheet
producers are covered by focused unit tests against a real realized
part and real std.materials records; the corpus-level golden
enrollment is the remaining slice. WO-101 Status stays `in-progress`
until (1) and (3) land.
