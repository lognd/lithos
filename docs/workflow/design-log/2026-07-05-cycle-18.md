## A. Decisions

**D84. The rule surface is adopted as proposed in D-B -- no spelling
deviations.** Each word, argued:

- **`rule <name>:`** -- the citable identity. The name is exactly
  what the settled machinery already cites: `waive dfm(<pack>.<name>)`
  (regolith/12 sec. 3), lockfile causes `dfm(<pack>.<rule>)`
  (regolith/03 sec. 2), E06xx provenance (regolith/09). Alternatives
  rejected on one-word-one-idea: `check` (a CLI verb and build tier),
  `constraint` (the sketch layer's `constraints:` block, hematite/02
  sec. 5). *Unambiguous:* a `rule` is the only declaration a pack
  block admits. *Intent:* "this is the shop's rule" is how experts
  say it.
- **`forall <var> in <query>`** -- an EXTENSION of the settled claim
  quantifier, said explicitly: `forall <cfg> [in <domain>]`
  (hematite/04 sec. F; regolith/07 sec. 1) already means universal
  quantification over a declared domain; the rule form quantifies the
  same way with a WO-08 entity query as the domain. No new
  quantification concept, no new word. `in` stays the registered
  domain-membership overload (hematite/04 sec. 5); the sec. 5 rows
  for `forall` and `in` are extended with the rule position.
- **`demand:`** -- argued against the standing near-name `demands:`
  (the regolith interface block; cuprite/07 sec. D, mech binds it as
  `tolerances:`). This is NOT a fit/fix-class accident: both are the
  required side of the one settled inequality the vocabulary already
  names -- `capability:` is "provider envelope, checked demand <=
  capability" (hematite/04 sec. 2A) -- so demand-family words enter
  the sec. 5 justified-overload registry as one idea. Reusing
  `require` was rejected: `require <Group>:` declares a group of
  individually-named claims; a rule carries ONE claim template whose
  citable name is the rule's own -- one word with two block shapes
  would be ambiguous at the reader. *Intent:* "the process demands
  this of every match."
- **`advise:`** -- the verdict-inert severity. `warn:` rejected as a
  mechanism word (it names the renderer's output class, not the
  author's intent); reusing `hint` rejected because `@hint` is
  already rung 3 (droppable VERIFICATION hints, regolith/12) and a
  rule advisory is designer-facing guidance -- same-word/second-idea.
  `advise` reads aloud ("the pack advises, the designer decides") and
  its INV-3 discipline (never an obligation, never release-gated) is
  stated in the track docs.
- **`resolves: <field> from free`** -- `resolves` is THE settled noun
  for a non-literal slot being pinned with a cause: regolith/03
  sec. 2 is titled "Resolution and the lockfile", the lockfile rows
  are resolutions, the INV-21 API is Cause-typed resolution. Any
  other verb (`sets`, `decides`) would coin a second word for a
  settled idea; `pins`/`locked` are rung-2 HUMAN pins (the lock
  family) and a rule resolving `free` is machine resolution -- it
  must NOT borrow the lock family's word. Near-collision audit
  against `realizes` (cuprite workloads): different domains that
  never share a statement position, full-word distance, each the
  established term of its own idea -- passes the fit/fix test.
  `from free` reuses two existing words at their exact meanings:
  `free` IS the value source being resolved (regolith/03), and
  `from` is the registered "begin from this basis" overload (stage
  `from=` / walk `from <datum>`), extended in sec. 5 with this
  position.
- **`per:`** -- the citation. Engineering register: "per
  MIL-STD-810G" / "per IPC-2221B" is immovable jargon for "according
  to", which is the intent (D-H: the expert's provenance survives
  into the error message). String-valued field position makes it
  unambiguous. Rejected: `source:` (collides head-on with the
  value-SOURCE vocabulary -- a real ambiguity, mantra 1), `ref:`
  (already means registry references, `sn_curve(ref=...)`), `cite:`
  (not how engineers annotate).
- **`why:`** -- the one-line physical reason; it IS the E06xx
  explanation text. `reason:` says the same thing longer
  (frequently-typed words are short and read aloud -- vocabulary
  principle 3); `note:` rejected because regolith/03 sec. 4 already
  emits a `note:` for boundary-pinned resolutions (second idea);
  `message:` is a mechanism word.
- **`expect:` with `pass:` / `fail:`** -- in-pack fixtures, the
  test-driven authoring loop (D-H). `expect` states the author's
  intent and is untaken. `test:` rejected: `by test(<ref>)` is
  evidence provenance (hematite/04 sec. F) -- same word, different
  idea. `examples:` rejected as weaker than the semantics (the cases
  MUST hold; `rules test` runs them). `pass`/`fail` are
  position-unambiguous field keys inside `expect:` only.

**D85. No new lexer keywords: the entire rule surface is contextual.**
`process` appears as `process=<module>` in every stage header of the
existing corpus, and the rule words (`rule`, `forall`, `demand`,
`advise`, `resolves`, `per`, `why`, `expect`, `pass`, `fail`,
`capability`, `dfm`, `drc`, `erc`) occur -- or may occur -- as
ordinary identifiers and path segments in value position
(`capability.min_bend_ratio`, `dfm(rule)` in waive targets).
Promoting any of them to lexer keywords would re-kind tokens across
the corpus and break path parsing -- the exact failure the parser's
block-word idiom exists to avoid (`block_intro_node`, parser.rs). The
rule words are therefore recognized only at statement-start inside
their enclosing context, nested: a `process` decl body admits
`capability:` and the pack blocks; a pack block admits `rule` decls;
a rule body admits the seven fields; an `expect:` body admits
`pass:`/`fail:` cases. A coincidental `dfm: 5` field in a part body
or a `rule = x` ctor is never mis-promoted. *Unambiguous by
position; zero corpus churn.*

**D86. The D-B2 discipline boundary is normative in the track docs.**
cuprite/04 sec. 4 now states it where DRC/ERC is specified: the v1
net discipline (terminal ledger, reference reachability, one
voltage-imposer, supply-short, single-driver with `arbitrate` joins)
is CORE LANGUAGE SEMANTICS in the E03xx family -- it fires with no
pack attached, cannot be detached, and is not waivable; the
intentional escape is the in-language construct (`arbitrate`,
declared joins), never an override; packs EXTEND the floor and must
not restate it (NO DUPLICATION). hematite/02 sec. 10 states the mech
analog (ownership/borrow conflicts and capability violations are not
rules).

## B. Findings

**F93. The corpus's `process=` referents are phantoms, so the grammar
lands free.** No file under `examples/` authors a top-level `process`
body (grep: only `process=` stage-header uses). The typed grammar
therefore changes nothing in the parsed corpus, the golden hashes, or
the snapshot set for existing files; the reference packs that
un-phantom `jlc_2l`/`cnc_mill`/`sheet_metal` are WO-28 deliverable 6
(engine wave).

**F94. The owner-corrected expression surface needs NO grammar
widening.** Current-driven fanout (`demand: sum(n.loads.i_input) <=
n.driver.i_drive`) is, syntactically, a comparison of a `CallExpr`
over a dotted-path argument against a dotted `Path` -- shapes the
Pratt claim-expression grammar has parsed since WO-05. Aggregates
over query results and registry-record dereference are SEMANTIC
capabilities of the engine wave (folding, vendor-LUT resolution,
corner evaluation); the parse surface was already closed. Proved by
`rule_demand_parses_aggregate_and_record_dereference` (parser tests).

**F95. One honest parse residue, recorded not hidden.** Boolean
connectives inside query filters (`bends.where(not b.at_free_edge)`)
are not modeled by the value grammar; a `forall` clause parses its
query with the expression grammar and sweeps any unmodeled tail
losslessly INSIDE the clause node (the `GenericParams`/
`WorkloadParams` "structure recorded, not further decomposed" idiom).
Nothing errors, nothing is invented; the engine wave reads the query
text back off the clause (as WO-08 queries are resolved today).
